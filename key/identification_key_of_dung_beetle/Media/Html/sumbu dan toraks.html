TORAX STRUCTURES

1. Axis (Body Direction)
	Anterior				: Direction toward the head (front).
	Posterior				: Direction toward the tail/rear (back).
	Dorsal				: The back side (top).
	Ventral				: The belly side (bottom).
	Lateral				: The side part (right or left).
	Apical				: The tip part furthest from the base of the body.
	Basal				: The base part closest to the body.

2. Thorax (The chest part or the second body part of an insect)
	Forelegs				: Front leg of an insect.
	Midlegs				: Middle leg of an insect.
	Hindlegs				: Back leg of an insect.
	Femur        			: Thigh of an insect leg (the part that is usually the largest).
	Tibia       				: Calf or shin of an insect leg.
	Pronotum			: Top hard skin plate covering the first chest segment.
	Pronotal Protuberance	: Bump structure present on the front thorax.
	Elytra       			: Front wings of beetles that have hardened to become like a protective shield.
	Scutellum     			: A small triangular-shaped part wedged between the base of the elytra.
