Identification Keys of Dung Beetle in Oil Palm Plantations Riau

Description

Dung beetles (Coleoptera: Scarabaeidae) play an important role in maintaining ecosystem balance. Their behavior of utilizing mammalian feces can act as decomposers and bioindicators of natural habitat damage. Dung beetles are divided into two main subfamilies, Scarabaeinae and Aphodiinae. The Scarabaeinae subfamily is a type of true dung beetle that actively carries dung (rollers) and bury dung in underground chambers (tunnelers). The Aphodiinae subfamily is a type of dung beetle that lives in dung (dwellers). These two subfamilies have almost the same morphological character, so accurate species identification is important to recognize differences and group them according to taxa. Identification can be done through a determination key, namely comparing one matching characteristic and then continuing with other characteristics. The large number of characters in each key makes the number of keys large, requiring less time and high accuracy. Faster and more accurate identification can be achieved through computer-based methods using Lucid. Lucid is software for creating identification keys by selecting multiple options without having to follow the key sequence. The Lucid-based identification key provides a more efficient and user-friendly identification process through the use of images and multi-access character selection.
